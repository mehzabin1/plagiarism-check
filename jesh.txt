The French Revolution was a major political and social upheaval in France that lasted from **1789 to 1799**. It transformed France from an absolute monarchy into a republic and had a profound impact on the world.

### Causes of the French Revolution

1. **Social Inequality**

   * French society was divided into three estates:

     * First Estate: Clergy
     * Second Estate: Nobility
     * Third Estate: Common people (peasants, workers, and the middle class)
   * The Third Estate paid most taxes but had little political power.

2. **Economic Problems**

   * France faced severe debt due to wars and extravagant spending by the monarchy.
   * Poor harvests led to food shortages and rising bread prices.

3. **Absolute Monarchy**

   * King Louis XVI held absolute power, and many people were unhappy with his rule.

4. **Influence of Enlightenment Ideas**

   * Thinkers like Jean-Jacques Rousseau and Voltaire promoted ideas of liberty, equality, and democracy.

1. **1789 – Estates-General Meeting**

   * Representatives of the three estates met to address France's financial crisis.

2. **Tennis Court Oath**

   * Members of the Third Estate formed the National Assembly and vowed to create a constitution.

3. **Storming of the Bastille (14 July 1789)**

   * Revolutionaries attacked the Bastille prison, symbolizing the end of royal oppression.

4. **Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen**

   * Proclaimed freedom, equality, and popular sovereignty.

5. **Reign of Terror (1793–1794)**

   * Led by Maximilien Robespierre, thousands of suspected enemies of the revolution were executed.

6. **Execution of the King**

   * Louis XVI was executed in 1793, ending the monarchy.

### Results of the Revolution

* End of absolute monarchy in France.
* Rise of democratic and nationalist ideas.
* Abolition of feudal privileges.
* Greater emphasis on equality before the law.
* Political instability eventually led to the rise of Napoleon Bonaparte.

### Significance

The French Revolution spread the ideals of **Liberty, Equality, and Fraternity** throughout Europe and inspired later movements for democracy and human rights around the world.

**In one sentence:** The French Revolution was a struggle against inequality and absolute monarchy that reshaped France and influenced modern democratic societies.
