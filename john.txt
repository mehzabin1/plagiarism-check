# The French Revolution (1789–1799)

The French Revolution was a period of radical political and social upheaval in France that fundamentally transformed the country and influenced the modern world.

## Causes

**1. Social Inequality**
French society was divided into three estates:
- **First Estate**: Clergy (~0.5% of population)
- **Second Estate**: Nobility (~1.5%)
- **Third Estate**: Commoners (~98%) — peasants, workers, and the bourgeoisie, who bore most of the tax burden

**2. Economic Crisis**
- Massive government debt from wars (including aid to American Revolution)
- Poor harvests in 1788 led to bread shortages and high prices
- Unfair taxation system

**3. Political Weakness**
- King Louis XVI was indecisive and out of touch
- Absolute monarchy resisted reform

**4. Enlightenment Ideas**
- Thinkers like Voltaire, Rousseau, and Montesquieu inspired ideas of liberty, equality, and popular sovereignty

## Key Events

- **May 1789**: Estates-General convened
- **June 1789**: Third Estate formed the **National Assembly** and took the **Tennis Court Oath**
- **July 14, 1789**: **Storming of the Bastille** — symbolic start of the Revolution
- **August 1789**: **Declaration of the Rights of Man and of the Citizen**
- **1791**: Constitutional monarchy established
- **1792**: Monarchy abolished; First French Republic declared
- **1793**: King Louis XVI executed by guillotine
- **1793–1794**: **Reign of Terror** under Robespierre — thousands executed
- **1794**: Robespierre overthrown and executed
- **1795–1799**: Rule of the Directory
- **1799**: **Napoleon Bonaparte** seized power in a coup, ending the Revolution

## Major Outcomes

- End of absolute monarchy and feudal privileges in France
- Rise of nationalism and citizenship
- Spread of revolutionary ideals: **Liberty, Equality, Fraternity**
- Inspired revolutions worldwide (Haiti, Latin America, Europe)
- Paved the way for the Napoleonic Era

## Legacy
